# OpenTPI Community Badges

OpenTPI Community Badges are designed to recognize and encourage
meaningful participation in our open source community.

> **⚠️ Note on Badge Display:**
> These badges are an internal OpenTPI community recognition and are **not official GitHub Profile Achievements**. 
> Badges awarded will be showcased in our official [`Community_Badges.md`](./Community_Badges.md). They do not automatically sync to your personal GitHub Profile, but you are welcome to paste the badge snippets into your own profile to show off your impact!

Whether you're asking questions, helping others, sharing insights,
or building real-world projects — every contribution matters 💛

Badges are awarded by OpenTPI maintainers based on community impact
and engagement. This is **not a competition**, but a way to celebrate
learning, sharing, and growing together.

---

## 🟢 Participation Badges

### 1. First Step Badge
**Your open source journey starts here.**
[First Step Badge](./Badges_Assets/01-First%20Step%20Badge.svg)

**Trigger**
- Your first post or thread in GitHub Discussions

**Purpose**
- Reduce intimidation for newcomers
- Encourage the first interaction with the community

**Examples**
- Asking a beginner question
- Starting a discussion about open source topics

---

## 🔵 Implementation & Representative Contributions

### 2. Project Builder Badge
**Building, sharing, and learning — this is open source.**
[Project Builder Badge](./Badges_Assets/02-Project%20Builder%20Badge.svg)

**Trigger**
- Using OpenTPI open source software (e.g. digiRunner) to complete a
  real-world application, and providing at least one of the following:
- a public repository
- a demo (video or live)
- a presentation or written explanation

**Purpose**
- Encourage hands-on learning
- Showcase real-world use cases and community creativity

**Examples**
- Course projects
- Community or student-led demos
- Integrations with other open source tools

---

## 🎖 Badge Awarding Process

- Badges are awarded by OpenTPI maintainers.
- Awards are based on **quality and impact**, not quantity.
- Badges are typically reviewed and awarded periodically.
- Most badges are awarded once per person.
- Project-based badges may be awarded multiple times for different projects.

Recipient lists are published in [`Community_Badges.md`](./Community_Badges.md).

---

## 💬 Notes & Community Values

- Badges are meant to **encourage participation**, not pressure it.
- There is no guaranteed timeline for badge awarding.
- Please avoid spamming or low-effort contributions for the sake of badges.
- Respectful, constructive interaction is expected at all times.

If you have questions about badges or believe someone deserves recognition,
feel free to start a discussion — we’re happy to talk 💛

Let’s build an open, welcoming, and learning-focused community together.
